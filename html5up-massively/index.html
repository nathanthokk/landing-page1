Who is
Nathan Thokkudubiyyapu?

Nathan D. Thokkudubiyyapu is an accomplished student at the University of Illinois Chicago, where he is pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a concentration in Software Engineering. Expected to graduate in May 2027, Nathan is distinguished as a Chancellor's Fellow, consistently earning a place on the Dean’s List. Nathan’s academic journey is complemented by significant professional and leadership experience. He has interned with the UIC Department of Computer Science, working on patent document generation using large language models. Additionally, he has served as a Client Support Specialist at Technology Solutions and as a Coding Instructor at Code Ninjas, further developing his technical and instructional skills. In the realm of leadership, Nathan has made substantial contributions as an Undergraduate Student Senator with the UIC Senate and as a Student Success Representative in the Undergraduate Student Government. His leadership extends to extracurricular engagements where he serves as the President of the UIC National Society of Leadership and Success and the Vice President of Professional Development of Alpha Kappa Psi. Nathan’s research experience includes his role as a Researcher with the UIC PAP STEM Initiative, where he has been involved in significant projects related to STEM fields. His technical skills are robust, encompassing proficiency in programming languages such as C/C++, Java, SQL, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and Python. He is also adept with developer tools including Git/Github, Visual Studio Code, Jupyter, Pandas, and NumPy. Nathan’s interests span Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Data Science, Software Engineering, and Leadership. His dedication to his field and leadership roles underscores his commitment to advancing technology and fostering professional development.
